What's the difference between cheap and premium epoxy? +
The main differences between budget and premium epoxy systems come down to solids content, durability, and longevity. Premium epoxy contains 100% solids plus polyurethane, forming a thick, durable coating in one or two applications that lasts 10-15+ years. Budget epoxy typically contains only 40-60% solids, requiring 3-4 coats to achieve comparable thickness, and typically lasts only 3-7 years. Premium systems resist stains, chemicals, and UV fading better, require less maintenance over time, and maintain better color consistency. While premium epoxy costs more upfront, it's a better long-term investment because you won't need to replace or refinish it as often. Contractors using Leggari or similar premium brands prioritize quality and durability over short-term cost savings.

What preparation is needed before epoxy installation? +
Proper surface preparation is critical to epoxy flooring success. The concrete must be thoroughly cleaned, ground, and profiled to create a proper surface for the epoxy to bond. Key steps include: (1) Cleaning and degreasing to remove oils, dust, and contaminants; (2) Grinding the concrete surface to open the pores and create proper adhesion; (3) Repairing cracks, spalls, or damage with concrete filler; (4) Testing for moisture to ensure the concrete isn't too wet (high moisture causes adhesion failure); and (5) Final vacuuming to remove dust. Contractors who skip or rush preparation typically see coating failures. Ask your contractor specifically about their prep process â if they can't explain it in detail or claim they skip certain steps, consider other options. Quality contractors budget 1-2 days just for preparation on a typical garage.
